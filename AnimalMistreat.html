A lot of people just go to zoos because they want to see new animals. What they don't know is that these zoos are mistreating those animals in very terrible ways. To help you know what zoos actually do to animals, we've come up with this list of the horrific ways that zoos treat the animals that they keep captive.

  1. The living conditions of the animals are not monitored very well.

    2. When someone reads this they might think that the zoos they go to have amazing habitats for the animals to live in. Those people probably have never been to a roadside zoo. These zoos don't have elaborate enclosures where the animals can feel in the wild. These enclosures at roadside zoos are basically cages with a concrete floor, only big enough for the animals to stand up, walk around a little, and lie down. The attributes of the cages are actually "fine"; the Animal Welfare Act only provides that animals have this sort of enclosure and inspectors only come to roadside zoos once a year.

  2. Animals in captivity can be driven crazy

    3. If someone were to go to a zoo, they might notice that some of the animals might be pacing, circling around or doing other repetativeactions. These types of behavoirs are symptoms for a conditon called "zoochosis." They begin to suffer from this condition because of severe psychic suffering. Many times zoos try to cover this up by giving the animals antidepresents or mood stabilizers like Prozac. Lonliness, frustration, and their small enclosures can all lead to zoochsis.
